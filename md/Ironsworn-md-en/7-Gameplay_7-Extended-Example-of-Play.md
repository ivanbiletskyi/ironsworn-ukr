# EXTENDED EXAMPLE OF PLAY

## KUNO TAKES THE STAGE

Let's see how Ironsworn plays in practice through an extended example. We'll follow Kuno, a character navigating the harsh Ironlands.

### Character Introduction
**Kuno** is a former warrior turned wanderer, haunted by his past. His stats are:
- **Edge: 3** (skilled in combat)
- **Heart: 2** (struggles with trust and connection)
- **Iron: 3** (tough and determined)

His current situation: Kuno has sworn an Iron vow to find his missing sister, who disappeared in the Deep Wilds three months ago. This is a **Formidable** vow (10 progress boxes).

### Opening Scene
Kuno stands at the edge of the Deep Wilds, where the trees grow twisted and the air grows cold. He's been following rumors of his sister's trail, but the path ahead is dangerous and uncertain.

**Player**: "I scan the forest edge, looking for any sign of passage or danger. I'm trying to be thorough but quick about it."

This calls for a **Gather Information** move, using Kuno's **Edge** stat.

## A JOURNEY INTERRUPTED

### The Move
**Player**: "I'll roll +Edge to Gather Information."
- **Roll**: 2d6 + 3 (Edge) = 8
- **Result**: Weak hit

**Narrator**: "You find signs of recent passage - broken branches, footprints in the mud. But as you're examining them, you hear something moving in the undergrowth nearby. Something large."

### The Complication
The weak hit means Kuno gets what he wants but with a complication. He's found the trail, but now there's an immediate threat.

**Player**: "I draw my iron sword and back away slowly, keeping my eyes on the undergrowth. What do I see?"

**Narrator**: "A massive boar emerges, larger than any you've seen before. Its tusks are jagged, and its eyes glow with an unnatural light. This is no ordinary beast - it's been corrupted by some dark influence."

This is a combat situation. Kuno needs to **Enter the Fray**.

### Combat Begins
**Player**: "I'll Enter the Fray, rolling +Edge."
- **Roll**: 2d6 + 3 (Edge) = 9
- **Result**: Weak hit

**Narrator**: "You leap into action, sword ready. The boar charges, but you sidestep its initial rush. You're in the fight, but the beast is faster than expected. You take -1 momentum."

Kuno's momentum was +2, now it's +1.

## INTO THE FIGHT

### First Strike
**Player**: "I'll Strike at the boar, aiming for its flank to avoid those tusks."

**Player**: "Rolling +Edge for Strike."
- **Roll**: 2d6 + 3 (Edge) = 10
- **Result**: Strong hit

**Narrator**: "Your blade finds its mark! The boar squeals in rage as you carve a deep wound in its side. Black blood sprays from the cut. You deal 2 harm and take +1 momentum."

Kuno's momentum returns to +2.

### The Boar's Revenge
**Narrator**: "The boar, enraged by your attack, retaliates with furious force. It lowers its head and charges, tusks aimed at your chest."

This is the boar's attack. Kuno must **Endure Harm**.

**Player**: "I'll try to dodge, rolling +Iron to Endure Harm."
- **Roll**: 2d6 + 3 (Iron) = 7
- **Result**: Miss

**Narrator**: "You try to leap aside, but the boar is too fast. Its tusks gouge deep into your side. You take 3 harm and -1 momentum."

Kuno's health drops from 5 to 2, and momentum falls to +1. This is serious - he's now **Wounded**.

### Desperate Measures
**Player**: "I'm badly hurt. I need to end this quickly. I'll burn my momentum to add +1 to my next Strike."

Kuno burns his +1 momentum, returning to 0 but gaining +1 on his next roll.

**Player**: "Rolling +Edge +1 for the Strike."
- **Roll**: 2d6 + 3 (Edge) + 1 = 11
- **Result**: Strong hit with momentum bonus

**Narrator**: "With desperate strength, you lunge forward. Your sword plunges deep into the boar's heart. The beast shudders, gives one final cry, and collapses. The fight is over."

### Victory and Aftermath
**Player**: "I check my wounds and search the boar's body. What caused this corruption?"

**Narrator**: "Your wounds are severe - you'll need to tend to them soon. As you examine the boar, you find a strange black crystal embedded in its flesh, pulsing with dark energy. This is clearly the source of the corruption."

Kuno has survived the fight but is badly wounded. He needs to make a choice about how to proceed.

## AFTERMATH

### Healing and Recovery
**Player**: "I need to heal. I'll use my healing herbs and make camp to tend to my wounds."

This calls for a **Heal** move.

**Player**: "Rolling +Heart to Heal."
- **Roll**: 2d6 + 2 (Heart) = 6
- **Result**: Miss

**Narrator**: "Your knowledge of healing is limited. The herbs help a little, but your wounds are too severe. You clear 1 box of harm but remain Wounded. The night will be difficult."

Kuno's health improves from 2 to 3, but he's still Wounded and will face penalties.

### The Mystery Deepens
**Player**: "While I rest, I examine that black crystal more carefully. What can I learn about it?"

**Player**: "Rolling +Heart to Gather Information about the crystal."
- **Roll**: 2d6 + 2 (Heart) = 8
- **Result**: Weak hit

**Narrator**: "The crystal feels cold to the touch, despite the warmth of your fire. As you study it, you realize it's similar to crystals you've heard about in legends - said to be fragments of ancient, corrupted magic. But as you hold it, you feel a strange pull, as if it's calling to something within you."

### A Difficult Choice
**Player**: "This is dangerous. I need to decide what to do with this crystal. Do I destroy it, keep it for study, or leave it here?"

This is a moment of character choice that doesn't require a move - it's about who Kuno is and what he values.

**Player**: "I'll wrap the crystal carefully and keep it. If it's connected to my sister's disappearance, I need to understand it. But I'll handle it cautiously."

### Continuing the Journey
The next morning, Kuno is still wounded but determined to continue.

**Player**: "I'll press on, following the trail I found earlier. I need to be careful in my condition."

This calls for an **Undertake a Journey** move.

**Player**: "Rolling +Iron for the journey."
- **Roll**: 2d6 + 3 (Iron) = 9
- **Result**: Weak hit

**Narrator**: "The journey is difficult. Your wounds slow you down, and the terrain grows more treacherous. But you make progress. Mark 2 boxes on your progress track for the vow. However, the journey takes its toll - you suffer -1 supply."

Kuno marks 2/10 boxes on his vow progress track and his supply drops from 5 to 4.

### Reaching a Milestone
After several days of travel, Kuno reaches an ancient ruin deep in the forest.

**Player**: "I've found something significant. This looks like the place where my sister was headed. I think I've reached a milestone."

**Narrator**: "Indeed you have. The ruins are ancient, covered in strange symbols that match the crystal's markings. You can feel the presence of old magic here. Mark 2 more boxes on your vow progress and reset your momentum to +0."

Kuno's progress is now 4/10, and his momentum resets to 0.

### The Discovery
**Player**: "I search the ruins carefully, looking for any sign of my sister or what happened here."

**Player**: "Rolling +Edge to Gather Information."
- **Roll**: 2d6 + 3 (Edge) = 10
- **Result**: Strong hit

**Narrator**: "In the heart of the ruins, you find a hidden chamber. Inside, there are signs of a struggle - scattered belongings, blood stains on the floor. But more importantly, you find your sister's journal. The last entry reads: 'The crystal calls to me. I think I can control its power, but something else answers. Something ancient and hungry.'"

### The Revelation
**Player**: "This is worse than I feared. She's not just missing - she's in danger from whatever this crystal awakened. What else can I find?"

**Narrator**: "As you read further, you discover that your sister was trying to use the crystal's power to heal the corrupted forest, but instead awakened something far worse. The journal mentions a ritual site deeper in the woods, where she planned to complete her work."

### New Vow
**Player**: "I need to stop her before it's too late. I swear a new vow: I will find my sister and prevent the ritual, no matter the cost."

This is a new **Dangerous** vow (8 progress boxes).

**Player**: "I mark the first box on the new vow track. Now I have two quests: find my sister and stop the ritual."

## LESSONS FROM THE EXAMPLE

This extended example demonstrates several key aspects of Ironsworn:

### 1. Fiction First
Every move began with clear fictional action. The player described what Kuno did, then chose the appropriate move.

### 2. Consequences Matter
The weak hit on Gather Information introduced the boar threat. The miss on Endure Harm left Kuno wounded, affecting subsequent actions.

### 3. Momentum is Resource
Kuno burned momentum to save himself in combat, showing how momentum can be a lifeline in desperate situations.

### 4. Progress Takes Time
The journey move showed progress isn't instant - it takes multiple moves and time to achieve goals.

### 5. Story Emerges
The combination of moves, oracle results (implied), and player choices created an unexpected story about corruption and ancient magic.

### 6. Character Development
Kuno's choices - keeping the dangerous crystal, swearing a new vow - reveal his character and advance the story.

## PLAYING WITH ORACLES

While this example focused on moves, in actual play you'd use the oracle to answer questions like:

- "What does the crystal feel like?" → Ask the Oracle
- "What kind of ruins are these?" → Location Oracle
- "Who else might be here?" → Character Oracle

The oracle adds surprises and depth that no single GM could provide.

## CONCLUSION

This example shows how Ironsworn creates compelling stories through the interaction of mechanics and fiction. Every roll advances the story, every consequence creates new opportunities, and every choice matters.

Whether playing solo, with friends, or with a GM, Ironsworn provides the tools to tell epic stories of struggle, sacrifice, and survival in the harsh and mysterious Ironlands.
