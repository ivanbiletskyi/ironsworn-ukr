# STRESS

**Stress** represents mental burdens and trauma. When you face mental shock or despair, make the *Endure Stress* move [page 95](3-Moves_5-Suffer-Moves.md#endure-stress). As part of that move, you reduce your spirit track by the amount of stress suffered. There are five ranks of stress.

*   **Troublesome (1 stress):** An unsettling incident or frustrating failure.
*   **Dangerous (2 stress):** A distressing incident or upsetting failure.
*   **Formidable (3 stress):** A horrifying incident or demoralizing failure.
*   **Extreme (4 stress):** A heart-rending incident or traumatic failure.
*   **Epic (5 stress):** A soul-shattering incident or the loss of all hope.

When you are opposing a foe, they can inflict stress (such as a terrifying visage or demoralizing taunt) based on their rank. Sample foes are in chapter 5 [page 133](5-Foes-and-Encounters_1-NPCs-in-the-Ironlands.md).

When you *Endure Stress*, you reduce your spirit track by the amount of stress suffered. If you are at 0 spirit, you are in danger of suffering a debility or falling into desolation.

You can recover spirit by relaxing when you *Make Camp* [page 64](3-Moves_2-Adventure-Moves.md#make-camp), finding fellowship when you *Sojourn* [page 71](3-Moves_3-Relationship-Moves.md#sojourn), or when you *Forge a Bond* [page 74](3-Moves_3-Relationship-Moves.md#forge-a-bond).

Unlike harm, you do not inflict stress on others—at least not mechanically. If you attempt to frighten or demoralize another character, make an appropriate move to see what happens.
